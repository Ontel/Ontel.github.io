How it Works
Share Client Requirements and Specifications
  • Tell us about your project and share your project specification documents
  • Provide an example C.O.P. that the customer likes, or we can help to create a gold standard if needed
Setup the Systems
  • An environment with your goals, requirements, and processes is then set up in Swift Projects
  • Depending on your deliverables, we may then start creating custom software applications to automate the processing and validation of key data deliverables like RETS and test results
Execute the Plan
  • We receive site information and scheduling and ensure any SOW specific items are identified in Swift Projects
  • We receive and review your raw files and advise on their accuracy and completeness
  • We compile and validate C.O.P., complete necessary documents, and upload to client portal
  • If rejections occur, we document them and incorporate into Swift Projects to prevent them from occuring in the future
Optimize Performance
  • We analyze performance by looking at key metrics such as how long it takes to receive raw data from the field, time taken to compile, QA, etc
  • We then present our findings and solutions and come up with a plan of action to optimize performance
  • This process is repeated at set intervals based on work volume and project timelines
Common C.O.P. Deliverables
We check all deliverables for completeness and accuracy and build systems to structure the process so that high quality is consistently represented throughout high volumes of projects. We verify whenever possible against as-built data (photos, tests, etc) or the applicable spec document, and advise on common punch item issues and best practices. Included in our price is the typical mini-C.O.P. package many carriers expect on either the same day of construction completion or soon thereafter. Here are main items we manage:
  • Fiber, Sweep, and PIM test results
  • Photos
  • Carrier and LL specific forms related with the scope of work
  • Punch-item correction tracking and submittal
  • Height Verification form
  • Antenna Alignment Tool form
  • RET Screenshots and worksheet
  • RF design and construction drawing red-lines
  • Certifications for equipment and people
  • Jobsite safety documents (JSA/JHA)
  • Log Book entries
  • Site clear confirmations
  • Material releases and bills of lading
